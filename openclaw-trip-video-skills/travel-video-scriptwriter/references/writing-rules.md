# Writing Rules

## Core Objective

Write short-video scripts that feel useful first and promotional second.

The audience should feel:

- "this solves a real travel-planning problem"
- "this is worth saving or commenting on"
- "the AI planner is part of the solution"

## Hook Patterns

Prefer patterns like:

- first-time mistake
- route arranged backwards
- same trip, wildly different budget
- one hidden constraint ruins the whole plan
- most people choose the wrong area to stay

Avoid weak openings like:

- "Today I want to share..."
- "Let me introduce..."
- "Here is a travel guide..."

## Agent Insertion

Insert the travel-planning agent as:

- route optimizer
- budget normalizer
- anti-mistake assistant
- quick itinerary builder

Do not write:

- hard-sell ad copy
- exaggerated income or savings claims
- fake urgency

## Visual Bias

Prefer scenes that can be shown with:

- city establishing shot
- route map animation
- budget comparison overlay
- itinerary card UI
- crowding vs smoother alternative
- attraction/food montage

Avoid scripts that depend on:

- subtle acting
- exact celebrity likeness
- hard-to-verify street interviews
- platform screenshots that may be unavailable

## Subtitle Style

- One main idea per line
- Short enough for mobile reading
- Match voiceover closely
- Rewrite for clarity instead of copying narration verbatim if needed
